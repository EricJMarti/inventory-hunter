EVGA GeForce RTX 3080 FTW3 ULTRA GAMING (10G-P5-3897-KR)

Witness the new era of gaming. The GeForce RTX 3080 is powered by the NVIDIA Ampere architecture and delivers epic performance that gamers crave for 4K resolution gaming. It's built with enhanced RT Cores and Tensor Cores, new streaming multiprocessors, and superfast G6X memory for an amazing gaming experience.

This EVGA GeForce RTX 3080 FTW3 ULTRA GAMING model is factory-overclocked to 1800 MHz Boost Clock (1710 MHz for reference card). It leverages iCX3 cooling which incorporates a suite of sensors and MCUs for smart cooling. And combined with a new streamlined design, brilliant ARGB lighting, and EVGA Precision X1 utility software for monitoring and tweaking, this next-gen graphics card is the definition of performance…and style.

Technology

EVGA's patented iCX revolutionized graphics card cooling from the inside out. iCX was designed to detect everything to cool spots and utilized interactive cooling to ensure Peace of Mind Gaming. With 11 patents granted and pending. iCX granted users control over the functionality and information related to iCX graphics cards via EVGA's Precision X1 software. Soon after, EVGA expanded the original design of iCX and greatly improved cooling with the second generation iCX2 and the brand new Precision X1 software.

At the heart of iCX3 is iCX Cooling, which refers to the baseplate, heatpipes, VRM and memory cooling, thermal pads, and fan design. The brain of iCX3, iCX Technology, detects temperatures at various points on the graphics card and adjusts asynchronous fans to provide the correct airflow for each area of the card.

EVGA iCX3 continues the evolution of iCX Cooling by providing improved thermal performance and significantly lower acoustic noise.

iCX Cooling set the standard

For a smarter approach to graphics card cooling and engineering to maximize cooling performance on the graphics card.

EVGA GeForce RTX 30 Series graphics cards with iCX3 receive an upgrade in every facet of the cooling assembly.

Goals of iCX3

Maximize the thermal contact surface area on graphics card components

Airflow pockets allow air to move more freely through the heatsink to remove more heat.

Patented copper base is 25% larger to cover both GPU and RAM area to improve thermal transfer and GPU/RAM cooling.

New 180° heatpipe design with selectively positioned heatpipes increases fin contact area by 65% and optimal heat dissipation.

Triple 2nd Generation HDB fans are installed on all air-cooled EVGA GeForce RTX 30 Series graphics cards to optimize airflow, increase cooling performance, and reduce fan noise

EVGA's iCX3 fans feature a 2nd Gen optimized HDB active motor for silent 0dB mode, asynchronous fan mode, and a special upraised “E” pattern that further reduces noise.

FTW3 center fans are offset by 10mm to increase the direct airflow area by 16%.

EVGA’s iCX3 fans turn off below 55°C, generating 0dB of noise

Smart cut-outs in the PCB and backplate improve airflow and reduce exhaust recovery. Micro-thin heatpipes on the backplate further pulls heat off the PCB

Built in Micro-thin Heatpipes on the EVGA GeForce RTX 3090 backplate adds additional heat transfer from the memory backside.

Carefully placed cut-outs in the PCB and backplate improve airflow and decrease exhaust recovery.

iCX3 vs iCX2

iCX3 Technology

iCX3 Technology combines sensors and micro control processors with improved iCX3 Cooling performance to make EVGA iCX3 the smartest and most-efficient cooling package available today.

EVGA iCX3 Technology leverages patented sensors and micro control processors with asynchronous fans to interactively-cool your graphics card. With at least 9 additional thermal sensors on the graphics card, EVGA iCX3 Technology can track emerging hotspots and direct iCX3 Cooling to beat the heat. Asynchronous fans cool only the areas that need cooling, which keeps your card quieter at all times. Through EVGA Precision X1, you can also monitor every iCX3 sensor and control each fan. With EVGA iCX3 Technology, you can let your graphics card worry about its own temperatures for a change and truly enjoy Peace of Mind Gaming.

EVGA iCX3 uses a patented 9 sensor design controlled by micro control processors to determine fan speed for the GPU and VRAM/PWM areas.

EVGA GeForce RTX 30 Series iCX3 graphics cards have 9 additional thermal sensors and multiple MCUs. All sensors can be displayed in EVGA Precision X1 and OSD.

Blue = Cool Green = Warm Red = Hot

iCX3 Technology uses asynchronous fans to cool graphics card components only as needed. These fans spin at different rates, ensuring that the correct areas of the card are cooled and reducing noise in the process.

Pros: Solid build. No sag. RGB Lighting looks great. Amazing out of the box performance (Mine ran at 2115mhz?! without any manual overclocking of any kind) AAA Games running at over 150 fps is incredible.

Cons: Very warm Power hungry 3x 8 PIN connectors makes it hard to look good without custom cables expensive!

Overall Review: Okay. So this card is an absolute beast. I ran the latest drivers (457.30 at the time) and experienced no issues in a variety of games. Witcher 3, Minecraft RTX, Outer Worlds, Red Dead Redemption 2, GTA V, DOOM Eternal. All ran smoothly without a hitch. Good impression so far! I use a 165hz 1440p monitor and some of those games maxed out my monitor specs with this GPU! The fans are all individually controllable (using EVGA's Precision X1 software) and gives a good level of control. They aren't totally silent, but they're quiet enough that in games with just speakers, I'm not bothered by them. The build is very solid and the card is fairly heavy, although it came with a sag bracket in the box which should help if necessary. (I was unfortunately unable to use it with my Phanteks P400A-DRGB case) in my case though, it sits straight up without it. As for the power connectors, it's a shame you need 3x 8 PIN as that third connector really looks bad with my stock PSU cables. (If you go for this card over other cheaper models, I'll assume you also care a bit more about aesthetics, in which case I highly recommend some custom sleeved cables). My only other issues (and I'm really just nit picking) are the fact the card runs warm, and while it's a testament to the efficiency of the cooler, my case felt like a mini heater next to me. You won't be disappointed by the performance or look of this card at all. Shame about the price though. If I had to choose between this and a cheaper model... I'd probably go for the cheaper model.
